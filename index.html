Ongoing Research

Digital Twin-Driven Fault Diagnosis via Inverse PINNs

Investigating the construction of an inverse Physics-Informed Neural Network (PINN) under Dr. Madhurjya Dev Choudhary, BITS Pilani to address sample imbalance in bearing fault diagnosis. Unlike purely data-driven generative models (like VAEs) which often lack physical interpretability, this framework embeds bearing dynamic equations directly into the network's loss function. This enables the accurate identification of dynamic parameters to generate physically consistent fault data whcih can later be used for robust fault diagnosis.

Multi-Agent Team Coordination on Graphs via GNNs

Aiming to solve the problem of multi-agent team coordination in graph environments with state-dependent edge costs. In these scenarios, agents take support actions to reduce traversal costs of risky edges for their teammates. To overcome the curse of dimensionality present in traditional Joint State Graph (JSG) planning approaches, this project leverages Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to learn scalable, decentralized coordination policies for autonomous mobile robots.
